**Lumière** is a free and highly customizable multipurpose Discord bot designed to help servers manage moderation, automation, and community utilities with ease. It includes moderation tools, purge systems, role management, counters, AFK handling, board systems, and fun commands, with more improvements planned over time.

This documentation explains Lumière's features, commands, and configuration options to help you use the bot effectively. Lumière continues to evolve based on server needs and user feedback.

---

## What Makes Lumière Special?

> Lumière is built as a practical toolkit for modern Discord communities, focusing on control, clarity, and flexibility.

1. **Moderation Tools**  
  Warnings, timeouts, bans, kicks with mass operations, case management, and moderator statistics with leaderboards.

2. **Purge and AutoPurge Systems**  
  Manual purge up to 1000 messages with 15+ filters, plus automated scheduling from 30 seconds to 30 days.

3. **Role Management and Automation**  
   Auto-roles for members/bots, persistent roles, temporary roles, mass operations, and join range targeting.

4. **Server Utilities**  
  AFK management, reaction boards with filtering, and live counters tracking 13+ metrics with goal tracking.

5. **Information and General Commands**  
  User/server info, prefix management, and troubleshooting utilities for permissions and configuration.

6. **Fun Commands**  
  Guess the Number game, mini-games, memes, jokes, rating commands, and interaction GIFs.

## Ready to Start?

Getting Lumière set up is simple and straightforward.

::steps

### Step 1: Invite Lumière

Add Lumière to your server using the invite link below:

::card
---
title: Invite Lumière
icon: i-lucide-bot
---
[Click here to invite Lumière to your server](https://discord.com/oauth2/authorize?client_id=1296095335483179080)

Ensure you have the **Manage Server** permission in the server where you want to add Lumière.
::

### Step 2: Initial Configuration

Once invited, proceed to [Initial Configuration](/en/getting-started/setup) to set up:
* Auto Roles
* Moderation preferences
* Command prefix settings

### Step 3: Configure Permissions

Review the [Permissions Guide](/en/getting-started/permissions) to ensure Lumière has the necessary permissions to function properly across all features.
